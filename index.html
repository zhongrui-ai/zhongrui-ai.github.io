I am a third-year Master's student at the National University of Defense Technology (NUDT), where I am fortunate to be advised by Professor Huimin Lu. My research centers on wearable exoskeleton and humanoid robot manipulation. Before that, I completed my Bachelor's degree at NUDT. During my undergraduate studies (2020-2023), I participated in the RoboMaster competition for four consecutive years and served as Team Leader.

My current work involves:

  • Ergonomic adaptation and system design for exoskeletons, encompassing both hardware and software development.
  • Building robot teleoperation systems for intuitive and effective human-robot interaction.
  • Investigating vision-language-action (VLA) imitation learning for humanoid robots.

I am deeply passionate about robotics, especially the challenge of building complete systems from the ground up and endowing them with intelligent behavior. My ultimate aspiration is to become an all-around engineer ‚Äî the kind who could rival Tony Stark!
